CAREER PROSPECTS

There are plethora of opportunities available after completing your engineering. It totally depends if one wants to pursue higher education or want to get employed.

Computer Science Engineer

  • Computer Systems Analyst
    • Computer Systems Analysts design information technology solutions that is required by particular organizations. They also test and maintain the systems.
  • Web Developer
    • Web designers design and build new websites for businesses and organizations.
  • Information Security Analyst
    • Information Security Analysts protect the information that is shared online and the security of network systems.
  • Software Developer
    • Software developers are responsible for designing, developing, and testing new software programs and applications.
  • Computer and Information Research Scientist
    • Scientists invent new approaches to computing, new types of hardware and software, and solve computing problems within various industries.


    Civil Engineer

  • Consulting Civil Engineer
    • Consulting civil engineers provide expert advice on the structure, arranging, and the management of construction projects, covering a wide range of private and business development. These engineers are the main force behind the development of streets, bridges, and offices.
  • Site Engineer
    • Site engineersâ€™ jobs include technical and organizational elements. Site engineers are part of the site management team. They are responsible for security, health, and safety, of materials and people.
  • Rural and Urban Transport Engineer
    • Engineers focus on the functional management principles that are applied for both urban and rural road networks and transportation systems.
  • Design Engineer
    • Design engineers are involved in the conceptualization, designing, and management of projects in various sectors.
  • Water engineer
    • Engineers from this particular field, repair, maintain and build structures that control water resources. They also take care of the disposal of sewage, and the prevention of flood damage.


    Mechanical Engineer

  • Auto Research Engineers
    • These engineers improve the traditional features of cars, and they work on aerodynamics. Their main job is to upgrade the performance of the motor parts and vehicles.
  • Robotic Engineers
    • Robotics engineers design, test, and build productive robots. Not only making robots productive but also to make them easy to use and safe and reasonable are also important.
  • Control and Instrumentation Engineer
    • Control and instrumentation engineers also design, develop, install, manage and maintain the equipment.
  • Automotive Engineer
    • These engineers are mainly focused on the development of different types of vehicles. They not only just maintain the existing ones but also design and produce new types of vehicles.
  • CAD Technician
    • These technicians make the designs using Computer-Aided Design (CAD) software of machines and other engineering products with the help of their IT and mathematical knowledge.


    Electrical Engineers

  • Associate Electrical Engineer
    • Associate Electrical Engineers support all design programs and analyze all circuits for all technical products.
  • Reliability Electrical Engineer
    • These engineers identify and manage the reliability and maintainability of all the electrical assets.
  • Senior Electrical Manager
    • Senior Electrical Manager provides a method for installing, designing, or maintaining electronic tools or devices. They also implement and maintain strategies, policies.
  • Professor
    • Professor teaches the subjects related to this field and they also research on the topics.
  • Maintenance Engineer
    • These engineers take care of industrial machinery and various types of equipment and tools.


    Aerospace Engineers

  • Principal Structural Analysis Mechanical Engineer
    • These engineers focus on the performance of the static and dynamic structural analysis and also develop and conduct tests that are required to evaluate design work.
  • System Safety Management Engineer
    • System safety engineers arrange, plan, and verify avionics systems.
  • Aerospace engineers
    • Aerospace engineers verify the designs of aircraft to check the standards whereas aeronautics engineers design satellites, aircraft, missiles.
  • Assistant Instructor
    • Assistant instructor assists the teachers in the classroom with the topics related to aerospace engineering.
  • Design Engineer
    • Design engineers are involved in the conceptualization, designing, and management of projects in various sectors.
